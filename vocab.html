Altcoin

An Altcoin is any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin. Examples of altcoins include Ethereum, Ripple, and Litecoin. Altcoins often aim to improve upon Bitcoin's technology or offer different features.

Block

A Block is a small piece of the Blockchain, similar to a page in a digital ledger that contains a list of recent transactions. When a block is completed, miners compete to compute a hash for that block.

Blockchain

A Blockchain is like a digital notebook that everyone can see and no one can erase; every crypto transaction is recorded on the blockchain.

Cold Wallet

A Cold Wallet is a type of cryptocurrency wallet that is not connected to the internet, making it more secure from online attacks. Examples include hardware wallets and paper wallets.

Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is a digital currency which you cannot physically hold; it exists online. The appeal is that you can send it to anyone, anytime, over long distances almost instantly.

Decentralized Application (DApp)

A Decentralized Application (DApp) is an application that runs on a decentralized network, such as a blockchain. DApps operate without a central authority and often use smart contracts to facilitate interactions.

Decentralized Finance (DeFi)

Decentralized Finance or DeFi means that no government or central bank controls certain financial services; they are run by code and computers around the world. The advantage to DeFi can be increased accessibility and reduced reliance on traditional financial intermediaries.

Encryption

Encryption is the process of converting information or data into a code to prevent unauthorized access. In the context of cryptocurrency, encryption secures transactions and controls the creation of new units.

Fiat Currency

Fiat Currency is traditional money issued by governments, such as the US Dollar or Euro. Unlike cryptocurrency, fiat currency is centralized and regulated by banks and governments.

Fork

A Fork is a change or upgrade to a cryptocurrency's protocol that creates a split in the blockchain. This can result in two separate versions of the cryptocurrency, such as Bitcoin and Bitcoin Cash.

Gas Fees

Gas Fees are transaction fees paid by users to compensate for the computational energy required to process and validate transactions on a blockchain network, particularly on Ethereum. Higher gas fees can result in faster transaction processing times.

Hash

A Hash is a cryptographic output (a fixed-size string) that protects each block of transactions in the blockchain from tampering. Hashes are created using hashing algorithms and are central to blockchain security.

Hot Wallet

A Hot Wallet is a type of cryptocurrency wallet that is connected to the internet, making it convenient for frequent transactions but more exposed to online risks than cold wallets.

Initial Coin Offering (ICO)

An Initial Coin Offering (ICO) is a fundraising method used by cryptocurrency projects to raise capital. Investors can purchase tokens or coins in exchange for established cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin or Ethereum.

Know your customer (KYC)

Know your customer (KYC) is a process used by financial institutions and cryptocurrency exchanges to verify the identity of their clients. This helps prevent fraud, money laundering, and other illegal activities.

Liquidity

Liquidity refers to how easily an asset can be bought or sold in the market without affecting its price. High liquidity means there are many buyers and sellers, making it easier to trade the asset.

Market Cap

Market Cap, or Market Capitalization, is the total value of a cryptocurrency in circulation, calculated by multiplying the current price of the cryptocurrency by its total supply.

Miner

A Miner is a specialized computer or participant that competes with others to compute hashes for each block of transactions in certain blockchains. The first to compute a valid hash and prove their work may receive a block reward.

Node

A Node is a computer that participates in the blockchain network by validating and relaying transactions. Nodes help maintain the integrity and security of the blockchain.

Order Book

An Order Book is a real-time list of buy and sell orders for a particular cryptocurrency on an exchange. It shows the prices and quantities of orders, helping traders make informed decisions.

Proof of Stake (PoS)

Proof of Stake (PoS) is a consensus mechanism where validators are chosen to create new blocks based on the amount of cryptocurrency they hold and are willing to "stake" as collateral. PoS is considered more energy-efficient than Proof of Work.

Proof of Work (PoW)

Proof of Work (PoW) is a consensus mechanism used by some cryptocurrencies, like Bitcoin, where miners compete to solve complex mathematical problems to validate transactions and create new blocks. This process requires significant computational power and energy.

Relative Strength Index (RSI)

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements. It ranges from 0 to 100 and is typically used to identify overbought or oversold conditions in a market.

Smart Contract

A Smart Contract is a self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement directly written into code. Smart contracts automatically execute actions when certain conditions are met, without the need for intermediaries.

Staking

Staking is the process of holding and locking up a certain amount of cryptocurrency in a wallet to support the operations of a blockchain network. In return, participants may earn rewards or interest on their staked assets.

Supply and Demand

Supply and Demand is the economic principle that the price of something is determined by how much of it is available (supply) and how many people want it (demand). More supply usually leads to lower prices, while higher demand tends to increase prices.

Token

A Token is a type of cryptocurrency that represents an asset or utility within a specific project or platform. Tokens can be used for various purposes, such as accessing services, voting on decisions, or representing ownership in a project.

Transaction

A Transaction is when someone sends or receives cryptocurrency. Each transaction is recorded in the blockchain.

User Interface (UI)

A User Interface (UI) is the visual part of a software application that allows users to interact with it. In cryptocurrency applications, a good UI makes it easier for users to manage their assets and perform transactions.

Utility Token

A Utility Token is a type of token that provides access to a product or service within a blockchain ecosystem. Unlike security tokens, utility tokens are not designed as investments but rather as tools for users to interact with a platform.

Volatility

Volatility refers to the rapid and significant price changes that can occur in the cryptocurrency market. High volatility means that prices can rise or fall quickly, which can lead to both opportunities and risks for investors.

Wallet

A Wallet is a digital app or physical device that allows you to store, send, and receive cryptocurrency. Wallets can be online (hot wallets) or offline (cold wallets) for added security.

Whitepaper

A Whitepaper is a detailed document that outlines the purpose, technology, and goals of a cryptocurrency project. It provides information about how the project works and its potential benefits.

XRP

XRP is a digital asset and cryptocurrency created by Ripple Labs. It is designed for fast and low-cost international money transfers and is often used by financial institutions for cross-border payments.
